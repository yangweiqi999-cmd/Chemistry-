Study guide — the mole & stoichiometry

Core constant

Avogadro's number & the mole

One mole = 6.022 × 10²³ particles (atoms, molecules, ions, etc.). It is the SI unit for amount of substance — think of it like a "dozen," but for incredibly tiny particles.

1 mol = 6.022 × 10²³ particles
Topic 1

Mole ↔ gram conversion (molar mass)

Molar mass (g/mol) = sum of atomic masses from the periodic table.

mass (g) = moles × molar mass (g/mol)
moles = mass (g) ÷ molar mass (g/mol)

Worked example — H₂O (M = 18.02 g/mol):

1

Find molar mass: H = 2×1.008 = 2.016, O = 16.00 → M = 18.02 g/mol

2

Mass of 3.00 mol: 3.00 × 18.02 = 54.06 g

3

Moles in 36.0 g: 36.0 ÷ 18.02 = 2.00 mol

Topic 2

Mole ↔ volume (gases at STP)

At STP (0 °C, 1 atm), any ideal gas occupies 22.4 L per mole.

volume (L) = moles × 22.4 L/mol
moles = volume (L) ÷ 22.4 L/mol

Example: 2.50 mol CO₂ at STP → 2.50 × 22.4 = 56.0 L

Topic 3

Mass percent composition

Mass percent tells you what fraction of a compound's mass comes from each element.

% element = (mass of element in 1 mol ÷ molar mass of compound) × 100

Example for H₂O: %H = (2.016 ÷ 18.02) × 100 = 11.2%  |  %O = (16.00 ÷ 18.02) × 100 = 88.8%

Topic 4

Empirical formula

The simplest whole-number ratio of atoms in a compound.

1

If given % composition, assume a 100 g sample → % becomes grams

2

Divide each element's mass by its molar mass → moles of each element

3

Divide all values by the smallest mole value to get ratios

4

If ratios aren't whole numbers, multiply all by the same integer (×2, ×3…)

Example: 40.0% C, 6.7% H, 53.3% O

C: 40.0/12.01 = 3.33 mol   H: 6.7/1.008 = 6.65 mol   O: 53.3/16.00 = 3.33 mol
Divide by 3.33 → C:1, H:2, O:1 → Empirical formula: CH₂O
Topic 5

Molecular formula

The actual number of atoms — a whole-number multiple of the empirical formula.

n = molar mass of compound ÷ empirical formula mass
Molecular formula = (empirical formula) × n

Example: Empirical CH₂O (mass = 30.03 g/mol), actual molar mass = 180.2 g/mol
n = 180.2 ÷ 30.03 ≈ 6 → Molecular formula: C₆H₁₂O₆
